Part 8:
Connecting everything!

In order for everything to work properly, all connections must be securely connected

Let's go through every component in the system

During this process, cable manage and keep it as clutter free as possible by using the vents in the case and zip ties.
Highly recomended to look at your motherboards manual to locate and connect all cables. Refer to this if you are ever lost, especially for the I/O cables.

  • Find the 24-pin cable, slide that through any cable routing recesses on the chassis and plug it into the corresponding 24-pin ATX power port on the motherboard.

  • Most PSUs have one wide cable that’s obviously intended for this slot, with no other connections on it.

  • It should plug into your board somewhere near the PCIe slots, but location will vary.

  • Identify your 8-pin EPS cable, and slide it up the back of the chassis and plug it into the 8 pin power slot at the top of the motherboard.

  • Make sure your CPU cooler is properly connected into a dedicated AIO cooler or CPU fan slot on your motherboard.

  • You will want to use the SATA connectors. These are 15-pin flat connectors with a dip on one side to prevent plugging them in the wrong way.

  • Route these from the PSU to the drive bays and mounts.

  • Next, we need to connect the storage drives to the motherboard, using SATA data cables. These are the L-shaped cables that carry data instead.

  • The motherboard usually has SATA ports located to the lower-left hand-side. Much like the power cables, route these through cut-outs and to the storage devices.

  • Connecting the front panel I/O, which includes the power switch, reset switch, HDD activity LED, and power LED can be intimidating but not as hard as it seems.

  • For the I/O panel these pins stand up from the motherboard, which requires a careful approach when plugging in the small connectors. The motherboard manual should provide information as to the layout of these pins and what they correspond to.

  • It’s important to note that any LED lights (HDD and Power), need to be oriented correctly, with the + and - cables installed into the + and - pins on the board.

  • It’s also a good time to plug in your USB 3.0 header, USB 2.0 header, and audio passthrough.

  • Audio is located on the bottom left of most motherboards. It will be labelled, and the pin outs will be different to the USB 2.0 headers.

  • Install your USB 3.0 cables (blue-colored ) into any available slots on the board, making sure to lineup the pins with the holes in the USB 3.0 cables.

  • Finally, high-powered graphics cards need their own power connection, usually in the form of a black rectangular connector with six or eight pins.

  • These plugs are colored and easy to spot, and only fit in the interior end of the card in one orientation.
