Part 3:
CPU Fan Installation

The second step is to install the CPU cooler onto the motherboard

Installation will differ depending if you have an air or liquid cooler

Many third-party coolers require installation of a back plate.
Follow the set of instructions given to properly secure your cooler and if any confusion comes during this process.
Not all CPU's come with a factory cooler, you must make sure you have one prior to making any further progress.

Thermal Paste

Air cooler

Liquid cooler

  • If your CPU cooler didn't come with pre-applied thermal paste, then you will have to apply a small amount on the CPU.

  • Squeeze out a small blob, around the size of half a pea, onto the middle of the CPU.

  • This will spread out once your cooler is mounted, and provide a sufficient amount of thermal interface material to successfully transfer heat from the processor die to the cooler.

  • Carefully orient the heatsink onto the pins or threads of the mounting plate and secure in place with any provided thumb screws or regular screws.

  • After mounting the cooler onto the CPU, try not to wiggle or twist too much, to ensure a smooth, full connection.

  • Lastly, plug the 4-pin PWM fan header into the CPU Fan slot on the motherboard and you're good to go!

  • Make sure to follow the instructions that came with the cooler carefully for this process. It is similar to installing a regular air cooler but with a little more work.

  • You'll want to attach a case fan onto the cooler radiator and think about where you want to place it inside your case.

  • Make sure where you want it placed doesn't get in the way of other components that you will want to install.
