Part 7:
Graphics Card Installation

The most imporatant component if you want to play games!

Graphics cards will come in different sizes, make sure your GPU fits inside your case.

Not every build requires a GPU, but if you have one follow these steps accordingly.

Part 1

Part 2

  • Modern graphics cards take up a PCIExpress (PCIe) slot. It’s a long, thin connector located on the rear of the motherboard, below the processor.

  • You'll first need to remove the backplate from the rear of your case, in order for the GPU to fit.

  • It’s one of a row of thin metal brackets on the back of the case to keep it sealed up.

  • Then make sure the switch on the slot on the motherboard is pushed outward.

  • First, line up the GPU to the PCIe slot on the motherboard.

  • Carefully insert the card and push down once it has made contact with the slot, listening out for a click of the latch securing.

  • Use the rear bracket screws to secure the GPU to the PC case for added stability.

  • If the GPU requires additional PSU power, connect the necessary cables to the motherboard.
